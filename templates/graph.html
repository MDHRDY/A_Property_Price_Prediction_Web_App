Despite the abundance of real estate information available on websites like Zillow, Trulia, and Realtor.com, tools for analyzing real estate market-trends are less sophisticated than other online tools, such as portfolio or stock trackers. Most real-estate websites highlight prevailing market conditions, but none assist investors or home buyers in comparing changes to one neighborhood against another over time.

Below is dataset highlighting changes to property values in New York City. All datapoints represent 1- to 3-unit residential properties. If you are familiar with New York City, you might be unsurprised by how much values have appreciated in Manhattan (if we also considered Coops and Condos, most of the island would be covered in red). The extant of changes in Brooklyn, however, is more astonishing. While wealthy neighborhoods close to Manhattan, such as Brooklyn Heights, Carroll Gardens and Park Slope, have appreciated at rates similar to Greenwich Village or the Upper West Side, other Brooklyn haunts, far deeper into the borough's interior than most hipsters have ventured, have also appreciated significantly. These areas could offer attractive investment opportunities for those that would prefer to avoid the buy-in price for Manhattan real estate.
